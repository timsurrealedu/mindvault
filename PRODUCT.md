# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People managing their mental wellbeing day to day — many while anxious, low, overwhelmed, or in
acute distress. Primary context is private, personal, often nighttime use on a phone or laptop.
They are not clinicians and not "power users"; cognitive load is frequently high and patience is
low. Some arrive in crisis and need the fastest possible path to relief.

The job to be done: *"Help me understand and steady how I feel, in a space I trust completely with
my most private thoughts."* On any given screen the primary task is one of: check in on my mood,
write/talk it out, calm down right now, make small progress, or get professional help — without
ever feeling judged, surveilled, or overwhelmed.

## Product Purpose

MindVault is an AI-driven mental health companion that pairs an empathetic AI with a privacy-first,
patient-owned data model: journal entries are encrypted on-device and anchored to the blockchain,
so a user's inner world stays theirs alone. It exists because mental health tools ask people to
trust them with their most sensitive data while offering little real control over it; MindVault
makes cyber resilience and patient-controlled data flow a felt, visible part of the experience
(Web3 Secure badge, smart-contract consent, on-chain anchoring) without burdening a distressed user.

Built for the *"Beyond the Horizon: AI-Driven Digital Innovation with Web3 & Blockchain for Cyber
Resilience"* theme — Health & Wellbeing Technology.

Success looks like: a user in distress reaches calm within the app in under a minute; a user trusts
the app enough to write honestly; and the privacy/security story is understood without a single
paragraph of explanation.

## Brand Personality

Calm, safe, empathetic. The interface is a sanctuary first — serene, uncluttered, and personal.
Motion is soft and breathing, never sharp or attention-grabbing (except deliberately, to mirror an
anxious state). Language is warm, plain, and non-judgmental; it speaks *with* the user, never *at*
them. Gamification and the pixel companion add gentle warmth and encouragement — playful is welcome,
manipulative or juvenile is not. Trust is conveyed quietly and confidently: security as reassurance,
not as a feature brag.

Three words: **serene · trustworthy · encouraging.**

## Anti-references

- **Clinical medical / EHR software.** No cold hospital-portal blue-and-white, dense forms, sterile
  data-entry feel, or anything that reads as a patient record system or paperwork.
- **Cold corporate SaaS.** No dashboard-cream default, hero-metric template (big number + tiny label
  + gradient), identical icon-heading-text card grids, or soulless enterprise tone.
- **Crypto / Web3 hype aesthetic.** No neon gradients, 3D coins, glowing hype-bro dark styling, or
  speculation energy. Web3 here means quiet trust and user-owned data — calm, not loud.
- (Note: lighthearted, playful gamification is **not** an anti-reference; it's wanted, as long as it
  stays supportive and never childish or nagging.)

## Design Principles

1. **Calm is the feature.** Every screen should lower the user's heart rate, not raise it. When in
   doubt, remove, soften, and add breathing room. The one allowed exception is the Anxious aura
   state, which mirrors distress on purpose so calm can be felt as contrast.
2. **Safety you can feel, not read.** Communicate privacy and cyber-resilience through quiet,
   consistent signals (encryption indicators, on-chain anchoring, consent you actively sign) rather
   than walls of reassurance copy. Trust is shown, not claimed.
3. **The user is always in control.** Patient-owned data, revocable consent, "stop anytime" exits,
   user-set pace in exposure quests. Never trap, nag, or dark-pattern someone who is vulnerable.
4. **Progressive disclosure under load.** Assume high cognitive load and low patience. One primary
   action per screen; reveal depth only when asked. The fastest path to relief (Panic Mode) is
   always one tap away.
5. **Warmth without weight.** Encouragement, the pixel companion, and rewards add gentle humanity —
   delight that supports the mood, never gamification that pressures it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** baseline: body text ≥4.5:1 (large text ≥3:1) in all three themes (One Dark,
  Gruvbox, Material); placeholders and muted text held to the same bar.
- **Reduced motion is mandatory.** Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support already in place; calming
  interventions (Box Breathing, the Aura, Panic Mode) must never rely on motion alone — pair every
  animation with text/state cues so the experience holds with motion off.
- **Distress-aware design**: large tap targets, unmistakable exits, no time-pressure or flashing
  patterns that could overwhelm or trigger; the Anti-OVT flow degrades gracefully.
- Theme choice itself is an accessibility tool: One Dark for nighttime eye strain, Material for
  high-contrast daylight legibility.
